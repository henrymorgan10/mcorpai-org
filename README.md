# ⚖️ Ethical AI for Humanitarian Contexts

> *"Technical excellence alone is insufficient. Legal security and ethical trustworthiness must come first."*
>
> — Gyumin Jeon (Morgan J.)

---

## 🌍 Overview

Most AI developers focus on building models with outstanding performance. Yet in real-world humanitarian settings, what matters far more is **legal responsibility and risk management**.

No matter how powerful an AI may be, if there is even a **1% chance of legal disputes** leading to claims of hundreds of thousands or millions of dollars, no NGO or international organization will be able to adopt it.

As the strength of GDPR demonstrates, low-income countries are not exempt. Technologies designed to protect vulnerable populations must account for complexity and risk in practice.

---

## 🧭 Ten Principles of Ethical AI

These principles form the non-negotiable foundation of this project. They are designed to ensure that AI can be confidently deployed by international organizations and NGOs in the most challenging environments.

| # | Principle | Description |
|---|-----------|-------------|
| 1 | **Minimize Hallucinations** | Incorrect outputs in sensitive domains—child protection, healthcare, disaster response—can have immediate and severe consequences. |
| 2 | **Ensure Transparency** | Open-source code and architecture are essential. Traditional AI's black-box nature makes trust impossible. |
| 3 | **Guarantee Accessibility** | Open-source AI must be simple enough for anyone to examine, adapt, and improve. Vital for sustainability in the field. |
| 4 | **Eliminate Data Exploitation** | No unnecessary data collection or centralized servers. A serverless design removes legal risk and reassures NGOs. |
| 5 | **Operate in Low-Resource Environments** | Must run on portable solar power and refurbished smartphones for deployment in the most fragile contexts. |
| 6 | **Remove Legal Liability** | Designs must protect NGOs and international organizations from multimillion-dollar lawsuits. Even minimal litigation exposure makes adoption impossible. |
| 7 | **Free of Charge & Lightweight** | Free access enables adoption in communities with no income. International institutions bear no financial or legal liability. |
| 8 | **Simplicity & Standardization** | Life-saving AI relies on reliable statistics and probabilistic analysis—not complex reasoning that increases hallucination risk. |
| 9 | **Non-Specialist Friendly** | Most NGO personnel are not programmers. Code must be straightforward, well-commented, and easy to adapt in the field. |
| 10 | **Zero Data Collection & Easy Deletion** | Users can erase the AI and its data at any time. Ease of deletion means freedom from responsibility. |

## ⚖️ Regulatory Alignment

This project embeds international norms **from the design stage**, ensuring no liability is transferred to partner organizations:

- 🇪🇺 **GDPR** — General Data Protection Regulation
- 🧒 **UNCRC** — UN Convention on the Rights of the Child
- ♿ **CRPD** — Convention on the Rights of Persons with Disabilities
